Signal vs. Noise in Your Own Life

January 15, 2026
personal-sovereignty decision-making

We’re good at filtering information—muting notifications, curating feeds, blocking distractions. But we rarely apply the same rigor to our own choices.

Every commitment fills your headspace. Every project demands attention. Every relationship requires energy. Not all of it matters equally, but we treat it like it does.

The hard part isn’t identifying noise in your information diet. It’s identifying it in how you spend your days. Most people can tell you what content is worthless. Few can tell you which of their commitments are.

If your commitments had an unsubscribe button, which ones would you click?
